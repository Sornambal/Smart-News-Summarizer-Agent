1. Meta Partners with UK Banks to Combat Scams

Meta has expanded its Fraud Intelligence Reciprocal Exchange (FIRE) program, a
partnership with banks to share threat intelligence and combat fraud. The
pilot, which initially included NatWest and Metro Bank, has already led to the
takedown of a significant concert ticket scam network and the removal of
approximately 20,000 scammer accounts. The program has received positive
feedback and will now onboard more banks to continue testing and learning. This
collaboration aims to strengthen fraud detection capabilities and create a
safer digital environment by sharing information and best practices across the
banking and tech industries.

Source: https://about.fb.com/news/2024/10/meta-partners-with-uk-banks-to-combat-scams/

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Meta Ray-Ban Display: AI Glasses With an EMG Wristband

Meta has unveiled the Meta Ray-Ban Display glasses, a new generation of
AI-powered glasses that allow users to check messages, preview photos, and
access information with a quick glance. The glasses feature a full-color,
high-resolution display and come with the Meta Neural Band, a wristband that
translates muscle activity into commands, enabling intuitive control with
subtle hand movements. The Meta Neural Band is the result of years of research
and can detect subtle movements, making it accessible to a wide range of users.
The Meta Ray-Ban Display glasses are now available for purchase, with demos
available at select retail locations in the US.

Source: https://about.fb.com/news/2025/09/meta-ray-ban-display-ai-glasses-emg-wristband/

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. Introducing Orion, Our First True Augmented Reality Glasses

A technology company has unveiled Orion, a pair of advanced augmented reality
(AR) glasses that aim to bridge the physical and virtual worlds. Orion features
a large holographic display, contextual AI, and a lightweight design, allowing
users to access digital information while remaining present in their
surroundings. The company believes Orion represents a significant leap in
human-oriented computing, offering an unparalleled form factor and immersive
experiences. With Orion, users can engage with digital content, interact with
others, and access information seamlessly, making it a potential game-changer
in the field of AR technology.

Source: https://about.fb.com/news/2024/09/introducing-orion-our-first-true-augmented-reality-glasses/

4. Introducing the Meta AI App: A New Way to Access Your AI Assistant

Meta has launched a new AI app built with Llama 4, a conversational AI model
that allows for more personal and natural interactions. The app enables users
to have voice conversations with their AI assistant, which can provide
personalized responses and integrate with other Meta features such as image
generation and editing. The app is initially available in the US, Canada,
Australia, and New Zealand, with plans to gather user feedback to improve the
experience over time. The goal of the app is to provide a more personal and
seamless AI experience that connects users with the people and things they care
about.

Source: https://about.fb.com/news/2025/04/introducing-meta-ai-app-new-way-access-ai-assistant/

5. Introducing Vibes: A New Way to Discover and Create AI Videos

Meta has released an updated version of its AI app, featuring an early preview
of Vibes, a new feed for creating and sharing short-form, AI-generated videos.
Vibes allows users to browse AI-generated content, create their own videos from
scratch or by remixing existing ones, and share them on various platforms. The
feed will become more personalized over time, and the company is working on
introducing more powerful creation tools and models in the future. The Meta AI
app remains a hub for managing AI glasses and accessing creative tools and
assistance.

Source: https://about.fb.com/news/2025/09/introducing-vibes-ai-videos/
