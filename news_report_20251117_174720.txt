**Executive Summary: Climate Change News**
The Earth's climate is undergoing a significant transformation, with the
average global temperature rising by 0.74 ± 0.18 °C since the mid-20th century,
primarily driven by human activities such as fossil fuel burning and
deforestation. According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change
(IPCC), this trend is expected to continue, with projected increases of 1.1 to
6.4 °C in global surface temperature by the end of the 21st century, leading to
severe consequences including sea level rise, extreme weather events, and
disruptions to ecosystems. Recent research has highlighted the complexities of
the Earth's climate system, including the impact of warming oceans on sea
levels and marine life, as well as the uncertain ability of the Southern Ocean
to absorb CO2. With overwhelming evidence confirming that human actions are
largely responsible for climate change, it is essential that the international
community continues to invest in research and works together to develop
effective mitigation, adaptation, and geoengineering strategies to address this
global challenge.

The Earth's average temperature has risen by 0.74 ± 0.18 °C since the mid-20th

species extinctions, and changes in agricultural yields. The international
