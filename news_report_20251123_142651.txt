**Executive Summary: COVID-19 Pandemic Update**
The COVID-19 pandemic, caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, was first detected in
China in December 2019 and has since spread globally, leading to a significant
loss of life, with over 2 million deaths reported in the European Region alone.
Although the World Health Organization (WHO) has determined that the pandemic
no longer constitutes a Public Health Emergency of International Concern
(PHEIC), the disease remains ongoing, and the WHO continues to monitor its
evolution. The virus affects individuals in varying ways, with most people
experiencing mild to moderate illness, but certain groups, such as those over
60 and individuals with pre-existing medical conditions, are at a higher risk
of severe illness. Vaccines have been shown to provide strong protection
against severe illness and death, and preventive measures like social
distancing, mask-wearing, and frequent hand-cleaning can help reduce the spread
of the disease.
Recent global trends indicate a decrease in COVID-19 cases, with a 16% decline
over a 28-day period, and a decrease in hospitalizations and ICU admissions by
40% and 31%, respectively. However, the number of deaths increased by 28%
during the same period, highlighting the need for continued vigilance and
monitoring. The WHO is tracking several SARS-CoV-2 variants, including one
variant of interest and seven variants under monitoring, with some showing a
decrease in prevalence and others increasing. As the pandemic continues to
evolve, it is essential to remain informed and take proactive measures to
prevent the spread of the disease and protect vulnerable populations. The newly
established COVID-19 Information Hub by WHO/Europe provides comprehensive
resources and integrated surveillance data to support these efforts.

1. Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) pandemic

The COVID-19 pandemic, caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, was first detected in
China in December 2019 and spread globally, leading to a Public Health
Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) declaration by the WHO. Over 2
million people in the European Region have died from the disease since its
start. The WHO has now determined that the pandemic no longer fits the
definition of a PHEIC, but the disease remains ongoing. A new COVID-19
Information Hub has been established by WHO/Europe to provide comprehensive
resources and integrated surveillance data on COVID-19 and other respiratory
viruses.

3. Coronavirus

COVID-19 affects individuals in varying ways, with most people experiencing
mild to moderate illness and recovering without hospitalization. Common
symptoms include fever, cough, and tiredness, while less common symptoms may
include sore throat and headache. Serious symptoms, such as difficulty
breathing and chest pain, require immediate medical attention. It typically
takes 5-6 days for symptoms to appear after infection, but can take up to 14
days in some cases.

Source: https://www.who.int/health-topics/coronavirus
